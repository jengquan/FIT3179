World Happiness Score 2019

Happiness is a healthy emotion which plays an important role in our life, that helps us to stay fit both mentally and physically. Happiness not only keeps us away from any health issues but also reduces our stress level.

Happiness is different for each person as everyone has their own way to be happy. Hence, it is important to understand and find out our own definition of happiness or what makes us happy as the reason of happiness may be different for everyone.

Therefore, the objective of this visualisation is to share insights on topics such as the Happiness Score across each country, the happiest region in the world, the distribution of Happiness Score for each regions in the world. Next, we will be identifying wheter Economy (GDP per capita) is a factor of happiness and wheter a happier country leads to a longer Life Expectancy. Both spatial distribution of each country's Economy (GDP per capita) and Life Expectancy will be identified in this visualisation. Lastly, we will also be covering the top 10 unhappiest and happiest country in the world to live in.

Top 10 happiest countries in the world ?

As mentioned earlier, we can see why Australia and New Zealand region tops the chart for having the highest average Happiness Score.

Next, Western Europe has the second highest average Happiness Score, this is because citizens in Nordic countries such as Finland, Denmark and Norway experience a high sense of autonomy and freedom, and social trust towards each other. Institutional quality and well-functioning democracy also plays a huge role in determining the country's happiness.

Besides that, we can see that Sub-Saharan Africa is the unhappiest region in the world. This is due to some issues faced by the citizens in countries like Nigeria, Tanzania and Kenya, where energy shortages, fuel scarcity, and the lack of access of clean water happens frequently. Not to mention, high crime rates and government corruption has also affected the overall country's happiness level in the region.
Based on the boxplots above, we can see that North America region has the largest range among other regions, followed by Middle East and Northern Africa.

This shows that countries in these two region tend to have a wide range of different Happiness Score as compared to other regions like Eastern Asia and Southern Asia.

Next, we can also see that Australia and New Zealand region has the largest median of Happiness Score, followed by Western Europe. The least median for Happiness Score will be in Sub-Sahran Africa.
