WORLD HAPPINESS REPORT 2019

Happiness is a healthy emotion that helps us to stay fit both mentally and physically. Happiness not only keeps us away from any health issues but also reduces our stress level.

Happiness is different for each person as everyone has their own way to be happy. Hence, it is important to understand and find out our own definition of happiness or what makes us happy.

Therefore, the objective of this visualisation is to share insights on topics such as the Happiness Score across each country, the top 10 happiest countries in the world, the average and distribution of Happiness Score for each regions in the world.

Lastly, we will be identifying wheter Economy (GDP per capita) is a factor of happiness and wheter a happier country leads to a longer Life Expectancy. Both spatial distribution of each country's Economy (GDP per capita) and Life Expectancy will be identified in this visualisation.

Distribution of Happiness Score across each country ?

Canada is one of the happiest country in the world as Canadians have strong social ties with people they associate with and it has a relatively low unemployment rate, a good health and education system.

A low level of pollution, high numbers of job oppurtunities and a good work-life balance of Australians easily contributes to the Happiness Score of Australia.

New Zealanders in New Zealand are well-known for its laid-back lifestyle and the social support obtained has contributed to the country's overall happiness.

Finland is the happiest place on earth as it has an extensive welfare benefits, low levels of corruption and a well-functioning democracy.

Norway offers tuition-free quality education and Norwegians have a strong network of family and a sense of identity which contributes to the country's happiness.

Average Happiness Score for each region ?

As mentioned earlier, we can see why Australia and New Zealand region tops the chart for having the highest average Happiness Score.

Is there a correlation between Happiness Score and Life expectancy ?

Do countries with better Economy (GDP per capita) tend to be happier ?

The bubble plot above shows us the correlation between Economy (GDP per capita) and Happiness score.

We can see that Finland which has a high Economy (GDP per capita) of 1.34 also has a high Happiness Score of 7.8, while South Sudan has a low Economy (GDP per capita) of 0.3, has a Happiness Score of 2.9.

Hence, countries with better Economy (GDP per capita) tend to have a higher Happiness Score.

The bubble plot above shows us the correlation between Happiness score and Life Expectancy.

We can see that Switzerland which has a high Happiness Score of 7.48 also has a high Life Expectancy of 83.8 years, while South Sudan has a low Happiness Score of 2.85, has a Life Expectancy of 57.8 years.

Therefore, countries with higher Happiness Score tend to have a higher Life Expectancy.

What is the Life Expectancy across all countries ?

Monaco has the longest life expectancy in the world due to the nation's state-funded healthcare system and their Mediterranean diet which includes seafood which are rich in nutrients.

Japan has a high life expectancy is largely down to the nation's healthy diet which consists of combination of high quality foods low in saturated fats and with few processed foods.

The accessibility of health care systems and nutrional education in all schools plays a huge role in South Korea's life expectancy.

The life expectancy of most countries in the Central and Eastern Europe region such as Ukraine, Russia and Libya falls between the range of 70 to 75 years.

Most countries in Southeastern Asia region such as Malaysia, Vietnam and Thailand has a life expectancy of 75 to 80 years.

A majority of countries in the Western Europe region such as Spain, France and Iceland has a life expectancy from 80 up to 85 years.
